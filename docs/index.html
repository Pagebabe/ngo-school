About the Project

Children's Home of Education is a rural education project built to help children who otherwise have limited access to learning opportunities. Many families here are very poor and children often spend their days helping with field work, caring for animals, or staying at home without education.

Our mission is simple: help the young Cambodian generation build a better future.

Why This School Exists

Director Wei Ha grew up in rural Cambodia without access to education. As a child from a poor family, he remembers: "Nobody gave me even one pencil."

He spent 15 years as a monk, learning Khmer, English, Thai, and Japanese. After working with human rights organizations, he knew he wanted to share knowledge with children who face the same struggles he did.

With support from friend Thomas Van Lienders (Belgium), who helped purchase land, the school was born. Today it serves 160 children who would otherwise have no access to English education or stable learning.

Life at Our School

These photos show our students, teachers, and the current situation of our school in rural Siem Reap.

The School

  • Free education: every child can join for free
  • Support: school materials, books, and sometimes food support when possible
  • Daily life: children learn, play, laugh, and build confidence

What We Teach

  • English (international language for future jobs and opportunities)
  • Morals / values (responsibility, respect, community)
  • Cambodian history (identity and knowledge of the country)

Current Needs

Right now the school is surviving, not stable. We urgently need basic infrastructure and materials.

  1. Rebuild the school building (currently wooden and unstable)
  2. Build toilets and improve sanitation
  3. Basic equipment (tables, chairs, books, paper, pencils)
  4. Support teachers (teachers work mostly as volunteers; current pay is very low)
  5. Garden and waste system to keep the school area clean

Our Vision

Our first focus is to make school number one strong and stable. When it becomes a successful story, we can copy the model.

In the future, we want to support students to continue to public school, reach university, and build careers that help their families and communities.

Without stable income, we cannot plan a stable future. That is why monthly support is important.
